Why it matters

Six ways to look at the same account.

Users

The wallet — not the blockchain underneath it — is what stops someone who's never touched crypto from receiving a crypto payment. tap removes it rather than making it prettier.

Web3

Chain abstraction is usually discussed as infrastructure. This is what it produces when carried all the way to a product: "wait, that's crypto?" instead of "cool, a blockchain app."

UXmaxx

The premise here is that UX, not another protocol, is what's missing from web3 adoption. Every decision in this product — down to the receipt screen — was made against that standard.

Particle

Proof that Universal Accounts in EIP-7702 mode can carry a complete consumer product end to end — send, claim, pay, withdraw, and an agent-to-agent x402 payment, same account throughout.

Magic

Headless 7702 authorization signing plus Google login is the exact mechanism that makes "signing in" and "having an account" the same event, not an aspiration.

Arbitrum

Every dollar moved in this project settled here, invisibly — the user never selecting a chain is precisely the experience Arbitrum's own ecosystem story is aiming for.
